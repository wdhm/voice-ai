# Atlas Airways Sports and Special Equipment Baggage

## General Rules

- Special baggage includes items exceeding **158 cm total dimensions**.
- If the item falls within your included baggage allowance, no extra fee applies. Otherwise, additional fees are charged.
- Special baggage must be added to your booking **at least 24 hours before departure** via "My Trips" or Atlas Airways Customer Service.
- Availability is limited per flight — book early to secure a spot.

## Specific Equipment

### Hockey Equipment

- Hockey equipment (bag containing sticks, pads, skates) falls under oversized/special baggage.
- Contact Atlas Airways Customer Service to arrange transport.
- Counts as one checked bag within your allowed weight and size limits.
- **Does my hockey bag and sticks go as one piece?** Hockey equipment typically counts as one piece of special baggage if packed together in one bag. If items are in separate bags, each counts as a separate piece. Contact Atlas Airways for confirmation on specific equipment combinations.

### Golf Equipment

- **1 golf bag with clubs + 1 pair of golf shoes** = 1 checked item.
- Free if within your included baggage allowance.

### Ski and Snowboard Equipment

- **1 pair of skis (or 1 snowboard) + 1 helmet + 1 set of poles + 1 boot bag** = 1 checked item.
- No personal items (clothing, etc.) are allowed inside ski bags.

### Bicycle

- Must be packed in a protective bag or box.
- Counts as 1 checked item.
- **Electric bikes** (e-bikes) must be shipped as cargo — they are not accepted as checked baggage.
- **Tandem bicycles** are not accepted.

### Musical Instruments

- **Small instruments**: May be carried as cabin baggage if within cabin size and weight limits.
- **Larger instruments**: Must be checked or may require purchasing an additional seat.
- Arrange transport with Atlas Airways Customer Service in advance.

## Overweight and Oversize Fees

- Fees range from **€45 to €135** depending on route and weight.
- Items exceeding both size and weight limits may incur multiple fees.
